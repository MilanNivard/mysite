Extended Chords

With extended chords things suddenly get very jazzy!

Most of these chords technically also contain a (perfect) fith, like most players however, we chose to leave them out to avoid the chords getting to cluttered.

The interval 9 is the same as a major second (2), just an octave higher.
The same goes for 11 & 4 and 13 & 6

minor 7th : 1, b3, (5), b7 (minor triad plus a minor 7th)
dominant : 1, 3, (5), b7 (major triad plus a minor 7th)
major 7th : 1, 3, (5), 7 (major triad plus a major 7th)

b9 1, 3, (5), b7, b9 (dominant chord with added b9, or b2)

min 9 1, b3, (5), b7, 9 (minor 7th chord with added 9, or 2)
9 1, 3, (5), b7, 9 (dominant chord with added 9, or 2)
maj 9 1, 3, (5), 7, 9 (major 7th chord with added 9, or 2)

#9 1, 3, (5), b7, b10 (dominant chord with added #9, or b3)

min 11 1, b3, 4, (5), b7 (minor 7th chord with added 11, or 4)
#11 1, 3, b5, b7 (dominant chord with a diminshed fifth)
maj #11 1, 3, b5, 7 (major 7th chord with a diminshed fifth)

b9 b13 1, 3, b6, b7, b9 (dominant chord with an augmented fith and added b9, or b2)

min13 1, b3, b6, b7 (minor 7th chord with a diminshed 11, or b6)
b13 1, 3, b6, b7 (dominant chord with a diminshed 11, or b6)
maj9 b13 1, 3, b6, 7, 9 (major 7th chord with a diminshed 11, or b6 and added 9, or 2)
